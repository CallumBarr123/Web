Meet the Team

Jamie Corbin

It honestly started because I tripped over a stack of mid-70s Japanese city pop in Jamie’s hallway and nearly broke my neck. His apartment had ceased to be a living space long ago and had just become a vinyl obstacle course with a sagging couch in the middle. I remember looking around at the teetering towers of wax and telling him, "We have to do something with this before you end up buried alive in liner notes." We realized we were sitting on archives of sound that deserved better homes than cardboard boxes on the floor. The moment we decided to stop just hoarding and start curating for other people, "The Record Room" wasn't just a cool idea, it became an absolute logistical necessity.

Tom Mc Gowan

Jamie likes to say the business started because of the mess, but really, it was born out of our 3 AM listening sessions where we'd dissect some obscure soul record until the sun came up. We’d spent years complaining that the online crate-digging experience felt so clinical and impersonal compared to the dusty magic of a real shop. We didn't just want to move units; we wanted to translate that specific feeling of handing your best friend a record and saying, "You have to hear this track." Building an online space that actually felt like a welcoming "room" to hang out in—that was the shared dream that finally pushed us to launch.
